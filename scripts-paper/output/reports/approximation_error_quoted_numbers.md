# Numbers quoted for the SDF approximation errors

Generated by scripts-paper/variance_bounds/quoted/run.R. Sample: quarterly, 1961-06 to 2026-06, T = 261 observations
(the full quarterly ACM zero-coupon yields and term premia; each quarter
carries its last available monthly observation, relabeled to the calendar
quarter end, so the final quarter may be incomplete and is included per
the pinned use_incomplete_quarters = TRUE policy; the typical-move
statistic below uses one fewer observation than T, its first difference).

Quoted in the main-text paragraph after the approximation equations and in
the Internet Appendix's Empirical Bound subsubsection and Table/Figure on
the variance bounds.

- max_bound_sdf_news = 4.188e-09.
  Largest, over the 39 quarterly maturities i = 3, 6, ..., 117 months,
  of the reported SDF-news variance bound: at each maturity the smaller
  of the fourth-order envelope plug-in (1/4) c_hat (k_hat + k2_hat), from
  compute_variance_bound(), and the first-order-cancelled plug-in
  (sigma_hat(q1_hat) + sigma_hat(q0_hat) + sigma_hat(g_hat))^2, from
  compute_news_q_bound().
  The no-Taylor q-arm is the smaller one at 1 of the 39 maturities
  (months: 3); the maximum is attained at 111 months.

- max_bound_expected_sdf = 3.194e-09.
  Same construction for the expected-SDF error: the reported bound is
  min{(1/4) C_hat K_hat, Var_hat(q)}, the fourth-order plug-in against
  the first-order-cancelled q-bound, from
  compute_expected_sdf_variance_bound().
  The maximum is attained at 108 months.

- max_error_sd_bp = 0.647 basis points (quoted as 'below 0.7 basis points').
  Square root of the larger of the two bounds at each maturity, times 1e4:
  the approximation errors are in levels of the SDF, so 1e-4 of the level
  is one basis point.

- typical_quarterly_move_one_quarter_yield = 0.00239 (per-quarter decimal).
  Sample standard deviation of the quarterly first differences of the
  one-quarter yield (the 3-month ACM yield, annualized percent divided
  by 400 to a per-quarter decimal).

- max_error_sd_over_typical_move_pct = 2.71 percent (quoted as 'less than 3 percent').
  Largest error-standard-deviation bound across maturities and both
  approximations, divided by the typical quarterly move above. The
  comparison uses that a one-quarter-yield move of dy moves the one-period
  bond price, and hence the SDF level, by approximately dy times a price
  close to one.

All bounds are plug-in estimates of population bounds, not finite-sample
guarantees; see the Internet Appendix's Empirical Bound subsubsection.
